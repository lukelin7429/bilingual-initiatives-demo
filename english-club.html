Student project

How we build a speaking board game

The point isn't the game — it's the English the children use while making it.

Write 20 questions. In pairs, students write English questions for the squares.
Check them together. The teacher reviews spelling and grammar with each pair.
Draw the board. Snakes, ladders and squares go onto a big sheet.
Colour it in. Teams decorate their board.
Play and talk. Land on a square, answer the question out loud.
Swap and play again. Groups trade boards and meet new questions.
